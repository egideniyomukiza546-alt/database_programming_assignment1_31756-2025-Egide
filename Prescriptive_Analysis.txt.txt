Prescriptive Analysis - MediCare Hospital
PRESCRIPTIVE ANALYSIS (What Actions Should Be Taken?)

Based on the descriptive and diagnostic findings, here are actionable recommendations
for MediCare Hospital to improve efficiency, reduce costs, and optimize patient care.

1. COST OPTIMIZATION – CARDIOLOGY
- ACTION: Implement a cost-review committee for the top 25% of Cardiology admissions.
- TARGET: Admissions costing over $15,000 (Heart Bypass, Brain Tumor).
- CURRENT STATE: Cardiology average cost is $8,866.67 (36% above hospital average).
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Reduce average Cardiology cost by 10-15%.
- MEASURE: Compare cost per procedure quarterly.

2. EFFICIENCY IMPROVEMENT – ORTHOPEDICS
- ACTION: Introduce enhanced early-mobilization physiotherapy protocols.
- TARGET: Reduce average stay from 7.75 days to 6.0 days.
- CURRENT STATE: Orthopedics has the longest stay and second-highest cost.
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Increase bed turnover rate by 22%.
- MEASURE: Track discharge dates vs expected recovery milestones.

3. RESOURCE ALLOCATION – PEAK MONTHS
- ACTION: Add an extra Cardiology specialist on-call during March and April.
- CURRENT STATE: March and April have 5 admissions each (25% of annual total).
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Reduce patient wait times and distribute workload.
- MEASURE: Track admission-to-treatment time.

4. TRAINING AND BEST PRACTICES
- ACTION: Use Pediatrics department as a benchmark for cost-efficient care.
- CURRENT STATE: Pediatrics has lowest cost and shortest stay.
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Replicate efficient care pathways in other departments.
- MEASURE: Share best practices quarterly and track cost trends.

5. PREVENTIVE CARE PROGRAM
- ACTION: Launch follow-up program for chronic condition patients.
- CURRENT STATE: Patient with repeated admissions cost significantly more overall.
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Reduce readmissions by 20–25%.
- MEASURE: Monitor 6-month readmission rates.

6. DOCTOR PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
- ACTION: Review high-cost doctors for efficiency optimization.
- CURRENT STATE: Top doctors generate a large portion of revenue.
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Balance cost-effectiveness with quality care.
- MEASURE: Compare cost-per-patient vs outcomes.

7. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE
- ACTION: Use department hierarchy for restructuring.
- CURRENT STATE: 3-level healthcare structure identified.
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Improve reporting and resource allocation.
- MEASURE: Quarterly organizational review.

8. PATIENT COST PREDICTION
- ACTION: Flag top 15% highest-cost patients using statistical distribution.
- CURRENT STATE: High-cost patients contribute large share of revenue.
- EXPECTED OUTCOME: Early intervention and personalized care plans.
- MEASURE: Track outcomes and cost trends.
